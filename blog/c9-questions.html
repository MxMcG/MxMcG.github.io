Asking Good Questions

Analysis of some of my own habits.

Tues, August 11th 2015

After reading Stackoverflow's how to ask page, I am now more aware of how asking great questions is a crucially vital aspect of being a successful programmer. Not only does asking questions help keep a group project productive and time efficient, it also makes the process of developing well designed code more streamlined. I have not asked many technical questions thus far on our DBC google+ group, however, I think that the examples shown on a site like Stackoverflow show how good questions can be answered if asked correctly and not previously answered.

In terms of asking questions about code online, I think the two most important things to keep in mind are, "Has this question been answered somewhere else online" and "How can I best format this question to make it clear and simple for people more experienced than I to answer?". Often times I find myself being a lazy researcher... using the first results that I get to implement important changes in my code. Just as in the medical profession, I think it is crucial to get second opinions about the issue you are having with your code from other devs who may have solved the issue differently.

In my question asking pursuits, I strongly desire to be more slow and patient in finding anwsers. Often times I find myself rushing to fing a correct answer or make sure that everyone is happy but I think that it is important for me to focus on being calm myself. Asking questions has always been a true strength of mine, in person at least, and I think that learning how to do the same in an online context will be extremely beneficial for my future.
